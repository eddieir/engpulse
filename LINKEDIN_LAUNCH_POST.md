## THE POST

---

I've spent years making sure software ships correctly.

QA, test automation, engineering delivery — my job has always been: find what's broken before it reaches production. Surface the risk. Help teams move faster and safer.

But there was always a version of the same problem that no tool was solving.

Leadership couldn't see it.

The CTO would finish their engineering update on Friday afternoon, translate GitHub activity into slides, write a plain-English summary for the CEO — and do it again the following Friday.

Engineers were shipping. But the story of what they shipped, what slowed them down, what was at risk — it lived in pull requests and commit histories that nobody in the boardroom could read.

I got tired of watching that translation happen manually.

So I built EngPulse.

It connects to GitHub with read-only access and generates a weekly plain-English leadership report — automatically, every Monday.

The report answers:
→ What shipped this week
→ What slowed the team down
→ What's blocking delivery
→ What leadership should ask engineering next

No DORA metrics. No dashboards made for engineers. No more Friday afternoon rewrites.

Just one clear Engineering Health Score and the answers leadership actually needs.

We're in public beta. Free to start.

If you lead an engineering team and still write your Friday status update by hand — this is for you.
